Perrsonal Interests

Thought

While studying at University of Arizona in Tucson, I became captivated by philosophy—specifically questions and problems in philosophy of mind, such as "How does consciousness arise from purely physical processes?" and in metaphysics, such as "What accounts for the intelligibility of the world?" I quickly learned that only very specific kinds of people were interested in these kinds of questions. Alas, here I am. After some time, I began to see that a spiritual perspective on reality was the only way to get satisfying answers to these kinds of questions. After quite a bit more digging, I landed firmly in the Christian faith. I've come to enjoy using these different perspectives—both philosophical and theological—to think about the world, culture, and life within them.

Action

  • Running is my favorite form of exercise. I enjoy the challenge of hurling myself into difficult situations and testing my ability to maintain.
  • Reading is my favorite form of productive relaxation. I am fascinated by the written word's ability to contain and convey such complex and profound forms of knowledge and wisdom. Of course, that's dependent on the mind behind them.
  • I'm something of a music junkie. I love both contemporary and classical music. A guy named Jon Hopkins is my favorite on the contemporary side—at least for the moment. Bach, more securely, is my favorite on the classical side.
  • I enjoy the creative and intuitive process of building things. As a fledgling web developer, I am coming to see web development as a wonderful medium for this.
